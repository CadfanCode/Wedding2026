Travel & Accommodation

Croeso i'r Gogledd Cymru!

North Wales is an amazingly beautiful area and has been voted one of the top 10 regions in the world to travel. However, travelling here can be a little tricky, so we’ve provided some details below to make it as simple as possible. If you plan on staying in North Wales for longer than a few days, we recommend hiring a car to make the most of what the region has to offer! Don’t worry if this isn’t an option—there are still plenty of places that can be reached by train and a few short taxi rides!

Travel Options

By Air

Manchester Airport: Manchester Airport is the largest international airport with car rental and train services easily accessible from the airport. The venue is approximately a 1-hour 45-minute drive from the airport. Train times vary from 2 hours 45 minutes (direct) to 4 hours (with changes).

Dublin Airport: If you want to avoid England or stop off in Ireland along the way, Dublin Airport is an alternative. Ireland’s largest international airport offers frequent ferry crossings to Wales.

By Sea

There are regular ferry crossings from Dublin to Holyhead (Isle of Anglesey) with crossing times varying between 2 hours 15 minutes and 3 hours 30 minutes. Ferry operators include:

  • Irish Ferries
  • Stena Line

Upon arrival at Holyhead ferry terminal, you can either take a taxi (45 minutes) directly to the venue or take a train to Bangor (Gwynedd) and then a taxi for the remaining distance.

By Car

We recommend using Google Maps, but if you’re still living in the 1990s, here are some details:

From Manchester

Follow the M56 and North Wales Expressway/A55 to Holyhead/Caergybi. Take exit 8A when you cross Britannia Bridge and follow the A5 towards Beaumaris. Chateau Rhianfa is located on the side of the road halfway between Beaumaris and Britannia Bridge. Caution should be taken when driving as the roads are narrow and winding, and the entrance to Chateau Rhianfa is easy to miss.

By Train

If you travel by train, we recommend going to Bangor (Gwynedd) and taking a taxi the short distance to the venue. We advise against taking the train to Llanfair­pwllgwyngyll­gogery­chwyrn­drobwll­llan­tysilio­gogo­goch (Isle of Anglesey) as trains usually only stop upon request—which isn’t helped by the obviously difficult-to-pronounce name.

By Bus

Don’t do it. Public transport in North Wales is notoriously useless (fel rhech mewn pot jam!).

Accommodation Options

Chateau Rhianfa (Isle of Anglesey)

If you really want to make the most of your time at the wedding and party, then we would strongly recommend spending the night of the wedding at Chateau Rhianfa. The venue has kindly booked off a few rooms for the night of the wedding. Parking included. So if you're interested in staying here then please contact us at: caiowain@gmail.com or contact Chateau Rhianfa and simply explain that you'll be attending our wedding.

Y Capel (Conwy)

As the name suggests, Y Capel (The Chapel) is a converted chapel in the center of the medieval castle town of Conwy. There are several fantastic pubs here (Albion, the Bank, Erskine Arms) where you'll probably find the groom after hiking in the mountains. There is also a phenomenal (but pricey!) fine dining restaurant within the town walls (The Jackdaw) and you can take a walk on the sandy beach of Conwy Morfa and enjoy a Michelin-recommended dish at Signatures restaurant. Nearby you can also visit the groom's hometown of Llandudno.
The guesthouse is exceptionally close to Conwy train station, but please be aware of the train times for your return journey unless you plan to take a taxi back. Parking is available although be aware that parking is very limited due to the very small size of the car park.

Coed Mawr (Rowen)

If you have a car and want the peace and quiet of the countryside, you can stay at the tranquil Coed Mawr Hall: a favourite of the bride. The building is a stylishly renovated manor tucked away in the Conwy valley. Here you can enjoy country walks down country tracks and enjoy a slower pace of life. There are no amenities near this location, so a car is a must to have a chance of reaching civilization.

Crusty AirBnB hosted by the groom's best man

Intending to stay in North Wales for more than just a few days? You may want to consider contacting the Best Man of the groom who has several AirBnB properties in the nearby town of Bangor, a short taxi ride away. Bangor is a student town that empties during the summer months. It serves as an excellent hub to explore Eryri (Snowdonia) or the West Coast of Wales.

Wedding Info

The Venue

The wedding and reception will be held at held at Chateau Rhianfa in North Wales. It boasts spectactular lounge rooms, banquetting hall and gardens with views of Eryri (Snowdonia), the Menai Strait, Llandudnos penisula and Irish Sea.

What is the dresscode

Wear your finest and we kindly ask that you avoid wearing trainers, except if you plan to change into them for dancing in the evening. Ladies avoid wearing white. Most importantly we want you to feel comfortable and to enjoy yourselves.

Is the wedding indoors or outdoors

If the weather is good, The ceremony will be outdoors. Otherwise it'll be indoors. However guests are free to wander the gardens or just enjoy the views.

Can I go for a swim the water?

The property is located next to the Menai Straits and swimming in this Menai is extremely discouraged. This section of water is known one of the most treacherous stretches of water in the British Isles.

Can I walk to and from the venue?

It is not recommended to walk to and from the venue as there are no footpaths and cars travel quickly along the winding road.

Is it okay to take pictures with our phones and cameras during the wedding?

Absolutely! You are welcome to take photographs after the ceremony. However during the ceremony we kindly ask that you refrain from taking photographs or films.

Will food be served?

Yes! Our aim is that everyone is comfortable and well fed. If you have any allergies or dietary requirements make sure to let us know when you RSVP.

Do I have to pay for parking?

No, parking at Chateau Rhianfa is free.

Is there a bar?

Yes, they take card and cash.
